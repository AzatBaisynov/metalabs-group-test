Moon

The Moon is Earth’s only natural satellite. It is about 384,000 kilometers away from our planet. The Moon does not produce its own light — it reflects the light of the Sun. It has many craters, mountains, and flat areas called “maria.”

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth. The Sun is a massive ball of burning gases, mainly hydrogen and helium, and has a surface temperature of about 5,500°C.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse environment, including oceans, mountains, forests, and deserts. Earth's atmosphere protects life and regulates climate.
